The Brief
Airtable

Building a Unified Marketing Operating Model Across the FOX Portfolio

The Situation

You joined Fox Corporation with a mandate to unify ad sales marketing across FOX Entertainment, FOX News, FOX Sports, and Tubi into a single, coherent portfolio value proposition. This is a newly created role with no inherited playbook and no existing system of record across the four brands.

Your Mandate
Unify ad sales marketing across all four FOX brands into a single portfolio value proposition for advertisers and the market.
The Operational Reality
Four brands. Four separate campaign workflows. No unified system of record. Airtable already runs Tubi and Fox News, the two most digitally mature brands.
Why This Is Relevant
You built a new media planning process at Paramount+ across a sprawling IP portfolio. You have solved a version of this before and know what good looks like.
You Have Solved a Version of This Before

At Paramount+, you led marketing across one of the most complex streaming portfolios in the industry: 100+ titles, a 140+ person team, fragmented by channel and brand. You built an entirely new media planning process to optimize paid, owned, and earned resources and a prioritization framework for resource allocation across IP. The parallel to Fox is direct.

The Opportunity

Airtable can be the system of record you build the global marketing function on: a single place to plan, execute, and measure campaigns across FOX News, FOX Sports, FOX Entertainment, and Tubi from day one.

1
Unified Campaign Planning
Build a shared campaign planning foundation. Establish a common structure for briefs, timelines, and assets across brands so your team stops working in silos from the start.
2
Portfolio-Wide Execution
Every campaign, from a tentpole sports broadcast to a Tubi original launch, runs through one system with full visibility from brief to activation.
3
CMO-Level Intelligence
With all four brands on a single platform, you get a real-time view of what's in market, what's in flight, and what's performing across the entire portfolio, with AI-powered insights and a unified agency coordination layer.
Questions Worth Exploring Together
  • How are you currently getting visibility across campaign status at each of the four brands?
  • Where is content being recreated across brands unnecessarily, especially for portfolio-wide advertiser campaigns?
  • How are campaign learnings and assets being shared, or not, across Sports, Entertainment, News, and Tubi?
  • What does your current agency coordination model look like, and where are the biggest bottlenecks from brief to activation?
  • What's your timeline for standing up a unified marketing operating model, and what tooling decisions are you making now?
We've Done This Before

Nike Global Marketing faced the same challenge: global marketing produced content that regional teams underutilized, the CMO had no unified view across campaigns, and an estimated $75M in annual content waste. Airtable became the global marketing hub, unifying campaign planning and workflows around key data sets. The CMO now has a global view from strategy to execution. Content adoption increased. Teams share learnings across regions.
